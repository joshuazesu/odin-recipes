Lasagna recipe

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian pasta dish made with layers of wide, flat noodles, rich meat or vegetable fillings, creamy béchamel sauce, and melted cheese, typically mozzarella and Parmesan. The dish is baked until golden and bubbly, creating a comforting, hearty meal. Traditional versions often feature a ragù (slow-cooked meat sauce) with tomatoes, garlic, and herbs, while modern variations may include spinach, mushrooms, or seafood. Lasagna’s origins trace back to ancient Greece and Rome, but the version we know today evolved in Naples, Italy, becoming a staple in Italian-American cuisine.

Known for its versatility, lasagna can be adapted to different dietary preferences, including vegetarian, vegan (using tofu or plant-based cheese), and gluten-free (with alternative noodles). Whether served as a family dinner or at gatherings, its layered structure allows flavors to meld beautifully, making it a crowd-pleaser. Pair it with garlic bread and a fresh salad for a complete meal, and enjoy the gooey, savory goodness that defines this beloved comfort food.

Ingredients

  • Meat: This super meaty lasagna has sweet Italian sausage and lean ground beef.
  • Onion and garlic: An onion and two cloves of garlic are cooked with the meat to add tons of flavour.
  • Tomato products: You'll need a can of crushed tomatoes, two cans of tomato sauce, and two cans of tomato paste.
  • Sugar: Two tablespoons of white sugar add subtle sweetness and enhance the flavour of the sauce.
  • Spices and seasonings: This lasagna recipe is flavoured with fresh parsley, dried basil leaves, salt, Italian seasoning, fennel seeds, and black pepper.
  • Lasagna noodles: Use store-bought or homemade lasagna noodles.
  • Cheeses: Parmesan, mozzarella, and ricotta cheese make this lasagna extra decadent.
  • Egg: An egg helps bind the ricotta so it doesn't ooze out of the lasagna when you cut into it.

Steps

  1. Make the meat sauce.
  2. Cook the noodles.
  3. Make the ricotta mixture.
  4. Layer the lasagna according to the recipe instructions.
  5. Cover with foil and bake.
  6. Let the lasagna rest before serving.
